Pitch

Hi, I'm Joara Paula co founder of Curves and I came to share educational knowledge, because education during the pandemic highlighted several scenarios that prove the need for transformation. To create new scenarios in which knowledge is democratic for all, being these one of the goals of the curves. For this, we will act in knowledge assessments, which make up the IDEB - Basic Education Development Index. Creating a way to evaluate, monitor, reward, recognize and multiply student knowledge. so that without commitment, inspire other students to do the same. For the institution and secretary of education, all data will be analyzed by capturing hits and misses. acting as a powerful decision support system, allowing you to correct students' difficulties and educators before assessments. Thus, estimating, improve the appendix and the indexes of knowledge. For all this to be possible, curves has a gamification strategy, which is very interesting in the current context of students. data collected in our validation, which guarantees the student's interest in curves. And we want to do all of this, because we are students who need new educational scenarios and we want to change the scenario current. To understand what we are talking about, Brazil is one of the five most unequal economies of the world in relation to education. The country's socioeconomic inequality is the third largest in the world in science and reading; and the fifth, in mathematics according to the Brazilian mail. One a good example happens in the communities of the city of Rio de Janeiro. At the Municipal School Judge Oscar Tenório, in Gávea, Zona Sul, the score achieved was 2.5, the worst in the state for the initial grades of Elementary School. At Colégio Estadual Lauro Sodré, in Higienópolis, North Zone, the data are even more alarming: grade 0.8 for the series finals. Knowing that the average goal by the IDEB (Basic Education Development Index) is 6.0. In addition to Brazil, curvas has a great chance of internationalization. The International Program Student Assessment – ​​is an international assessment that measures the educational level of 15-year-olds through Reading, Mathematics and Science tests. The exam is performed every three years by the OECD (Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development), entity formed by governments of 30 countries whose principles are democracy and economy market. Non-OECD countries can also participate in Pisa, as is the case with Brazil, invited for the third consecutive time. This whole scenario presenting, validates the importance of curves as a great business opportunity. Because in Brazil there are 1,309 education secretariats, of which 1,282 are municipal and 26 state, in addition to the secretariat of Federal District education. Where the estimate is of participation of 7,619,852 (seven million six hundred and nineteen thousand, eight hundred and fifty two) elementary school students and medium in 114,825 schools that do only the Saeb assessment. And for all this to be possible, we have a multidisciplinary team of students and educators in technology, pedagogy and with a lot of experience in rating systems, games and data sience.
