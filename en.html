Meet Curvas

A fun way to measure student achievement and promote access to the labor market.

How it works?

Concept

According to the International Student Assessment Program (Pisa), which assessed 79 countries in 2018, Brazil is one of the five most unequal economies in the world in terms of education.

A good example happens in the communities of the City of Rio de Janeiro. At the Desembargador Oscar Tenório Municipal School, in Gávea, Zona Sul, the score achieved was 2.5, the worst in the state for the initial grades of elementary school. At Colégio Estadual Lauro Sodré, in Higienópolis, North Zone, the data are even more alarming: grade 0.8 for the final series. Knowing that the average goal by IDEB (Basic Education Development Index) is 6.0. the country's socioeconomic status is the third largest in the world in science and reading; and the fifth in mathematics. Students with higher purchasing power scored 100 points higher than poorer students. Carried out through a test, the program is carried out by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD). Based on these data, it is possible to have a better view of the failures in education and thus take the necessary measures to improve the quality of education. Thus, Curvas will reduce bureaucracy through computerization, where teachers can save up to 34% of the time spent on bureaucracy, by automating processes, that saved time can be used to prepare attractive classes and ensure classroom learning! For students a fun way to take your assessment through gamification. In the near future, cur.vas will be a white label to be used in other evaluative methods such as internship selection and MEC concept.

How it works?

Curvas is a web app with all the data available in AZURE clouds, it works like a QUIZ-style game, based on the KUMON methodology.

Each student can have some support, to form their knowledge and meet some criteria:

  • Having 3 friends to help answer the question, the purpose of this is to create engagement among students and competition that encourages students. Each friend can only help once.
  • With classroom support, according to the question, a classroom repository can be used so that the student can read the content and get to the answer, teaching the student to learn, research and use their teaching materials.
  • Knowledge base, he can consult a repository that he creates, keeping some questions and answers that went wrong and was presented in the right way for this material to act as reinforcement.
  • Criteria - Curvas will only be released at break times, by the school or for classes that are not attending classes. Where, according to the degree of difficulty of the question and of the student, it has a resolution time. As the student learns or has difficulty, this time is better distributed for him to organize his time and have a plan for running tests.
  • Criteria - Student data will not be exposed to other students.

    • Curvas will be agile and simple, having only 10 questions, when the questions are correct, the student is ranked. The ranking will be by grade, by school, by educational region and finally by Brazil. Each ranking will give the student a different award and recognition, in addition to being seen by other participating students.

    Curvas Process

    Usability of Curves, based on project validation

    The usability of Curvas is very simple, the student accesses it at times defined by the teacher or educational institution. It is not possible to use it during class or at undefined times, for access simply enter your school identification data. The initial questions he chooses so that he gets the maximum score, answering only what he considers easy or knows how to answer (Kumon method).

    The Business Plan

    The business model is very simple, we know that the form of payment by government agencies is through licenses. For this, the amount to be charged per month will be based on the common factors of a license (cost). However, as a design business strategy, the first year of using curves will be a pilot in some schools.
